Questions

Things people
often ask

Do I need my own piano at home?
+

Yes — daily practice is essential to progress. A quality digital piano with weighted 88 keys is perfectly fine to start. Rachel is happy to recommend specific models for any budget during your discovery call.

How quickly will I notice improvement?
+

Most students notice meaningful progress within 6–8 weeks. Young beginners often play their first recognisable piece within the first month. Even 15–20 minutes of daily practice makes an enormous difference.

Are online lessons available?
+

The studio is based in the San Francisco Bay Area and lessons are primarily in-person. Online lessons via Zoom are available for intermediate and advanced students on a case-by-case basis.

What if my child isn't interested in piano?
+

Many of Rachel's most passionate students started out reluctant! The teaching approach for young children is entirely play-based, encouraging, and never pressured. The goal is to spark genuine love for music first — technique follows naturally.

How does billing and scheduling work?
+

Lessons are scheduled on a recurring weekly basis. Billing is monthly, invoiced at the start of each month. A 48-hour cancellation policy applies. Make-up lessons are offered where scheduling allows.
